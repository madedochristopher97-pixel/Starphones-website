The Ultimate Guide to Lipa Mdogo Mdogo Phones in Kenya (2026)

By StarPhones Team • May 1, 2026 • ⏱ 6 min read

Owning a high-quality smartphone in Kenya shouldn't be a luxury reserved for a few. With the digital economy rapidly expanding, from mobile banking and e-commerce to social media marketing, a reliable smartphone is an essential tool for everyday life. However, dropping Ksh 20,000 or more in a single payment is a massive financial strain for many Kenyans. That's exactly where the revolutionary concept of "Lipa Mdogo Mdogo" steps in.

At StarPhones, our Lipa Mdogo Mdogo (pay-as-you-go) financing plans are designed to democratize smartphone access. We allow you to walk away with a brand-new, genuine smartphone today, while spreading the cost over several months in affordable daily or weekly installments. In this comprehensive guide, we'll break down exactly how this model works, who qualifies, and why it might be the smartest financial choice for your next tech upgrade.

What is Lipa Mdogo Mdogo?

Lipa Mdogo Mdogo is an innovative asset-financing model tailored specifically for the Kenyan market. Instead of paying the full retail price of a smartphone upfront, you pay a fraction of the cost—known as a deposit. Once the deposit is paid, you receive the phone immediately. The remaining balance is then divided into micro-payments that you can pay daily, weekly, or monthly, typically over a period of 6 to 12 months.

These payments are conveniently made via M-Pesa. The smartphone is secured using software locks (like Knox for Samsung or specialized Device Lock software for other brands). This means that if you default on your payments for an extended period, the phone's functionality is temporarily restricted until payment is made. Once you complete all your installments, the phone is permanently unlocked, and you gain total, unrestricted ownership of the device.

The 3 Simple Steps to Get Your Phone

We've streamlined the process at StarPhones so you can get connected in under an hour. Here is the step-by-step breakdown:

1. Choose Your Device & Check Qualification

The first step is selecting the phone that fits your lifestyle. Whether you need a powerful camera on the Tecno Camon series for content creation, or a massive battery on the Infinix Hot series for all-day reliability, we have options across all price points. To qualify, the requirements are very simple:

  • You must be a Kenyan citizen aged 18 years or older.
  • You must possess an original, valid Kenyan National ID.
  • You need an active Safaricom line that has been in use for at least 6 months (this is used for M-Pesa payments and basic credit scoring).

2. Pay the Initial Deposit

Once you've selected your phone and met the basic qualifications, you'll need to pay the initial deposit. Deposit amounts vary depending on the total cost of the phone. For an entry-level smartphone like an Itel or basic Tecno, the deposit can be as low as Ksh 3,000. For mid-range devices from Samsung or Oppo, deposits typically range between Ksh 4,500 and Ksh 8,000. This deposit ensures you have some "skin in the game" while keeping the barrier to entry extremely low.

3. Make Daily or Weekly Payments

With the deposit paid, you sign a brief digital contract, the device is configured, and you walk out with your new phone. Going forward, you simply need to make your scheduled payments via M-Pesa. Most of our customers opt for daily payments, which can be as low as Ksh 60 to Ksh 120 per day. For many, this is less than what they spend on daily transport or tea. If you prefer, you can also bundle these into weekly payments (e.g., Ksh 500 per week). The flexibility is entirely yours.

Free Manufacturer Warranty: Just because you are paying in installments doesn't mean you get an inferior product. Every Lipa Mdogo Mdogo phone sold by StarPhones is 100% genuine and comes with a full 1-year manufacturer warranty. What Happens If You Miss a Payment?

Life happens, and sometimes you might be short on cash. We understand that. If you miss a daily payment, the device will prompt you with a reminder. If the payment remains unpaid for a certain grace period (usually 24-48 hours), the phone's screen will lock. You won't be able to make calls or access apps, though you can still receive incoming calls and access the M-Pesa menu to make your payment.

The moment you make your payment via M-Pesa, the system registers it and unlocks your phone almost instantly. There are no exorbitant late fees or repossession agents knocking at your door—the software lock manages the entire process seamlessly. Furthermore, if you get a financial windfall, you can pay off the entire remaining balance at any time before the contract ends, completely penalty-free.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Lipa Mdogo Mdogo?

Lipa Mdogo Mdogo is a smartphone financing model that allows you to get a new phone by paying a small initial deposit, followed by daily or weekly micro-installments until the device is fully paid off.

How much deposit is required?

Deposit amounts vary depending on the phone model. Typically, you pay between Ksh 3,000 to Ksh 5,000 upfront for most devices.

What documents do I need to qualify?

To qualify, you need an original Kenyan National ID card and an active Safaricom line that has been in use for at least 6 months.

What happens if I miss a daily payment?

If you miss a daily payment, the device will be temporarily locked. Once you make the payment via M-Pesa, the phone is unlocked instantly and you can continue using it.

Can I pay off the phone early?

Yes, you can pay off the remaining balance at any time before the 12-month period ends without incurring any early repayment penalties.
